\section{Aim}
The aim of the experiment is to study the drain and transfer characterstics of an p-channel Junction Field Effect Transistor. 
We shall forward-bias the gate-source junction, reverse-bias the drain-source junction, and use a fixed voltage bias method to construct our setup.

\section{Theory}
A Junction Field Effect Transistor (JFET) is a voltage-controlled semiconductor device in which the drain current ($I_D$) is 
controlled by the gate-to-source voltage ($V_{GS}$). The gate is reverse biased, resulting in very high input impedance.
\subsection*{Drain Characterstics}
Drain characteristics represent the variation of drain current ($I_D$) with drain-to-
source voltage ($V_{DS}$) for different constant values of gate-to-source voltage ($V_{GS}$).
\subsection*{Transfer Characterstics}
Transfer characteristics represent the variation of drain current ($I_D$) with gate-to-
source voltage ($V_{GS}$) at a constant drain-to-source voltage ($V_{DS}$). These
characteristics are useful for determining the transconductance of the JFET.
